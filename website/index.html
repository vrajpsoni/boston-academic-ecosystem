Academic Boston is not one campus. It is a network.

Wherever you move through Greater Boston, student life is nearby. Almost every mile across the city connects back to a university, student neighborhood, research center, hospital, or academic institution. Instead of feeling like a city that simply contains colleges, Boston feels like a city built around students. The map above highlights how deeply academic life is woven into the identity of Boston and its surrounding neighborhoods.

  • Longwood: medical schools, hospitals, and student housing in one corridor
  • Fenway / Kenmore: multiple universities and dense rental zones
  • Cambridge: Harvard, MIT, and affiliated research within miles

Scale is uneven, but the direction is clear.

Hover the chart to compare universities year by year. Northeastern and Boston University show the steepest long-term growth in total enrollment, while MIT and UMass Boston remain smaller in scale but central to the region’s academic identity. Together, these curves show that Greater Boston’s student population keeps expanding, shifting, and reshaping the city over time.

  • Northeastern: among the fastest growth in total enrollment since the mid-2000s
  • Boston University: consistently one of the largest student populations in the region
  • Harvard and MIT: smaller headcounts than BU or Northeastern, but global research anchors
